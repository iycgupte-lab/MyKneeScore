How it works

Beyond the joint

Your knee is part of a whole body

A knee rarely fails on its own. Weight, muscle strength, sleep, mood and activity all shape how a knee feels and how well it heals. Your score reflects the joint, but the guidance treats the person.

  • Strength and balance. Weak hips and thighs load the knee unevenly. Guidance includes whole-leg exercise, not just knee bends.
  • Weight and metabolism. Each kilogram lost removes roughly four kilograms of force from the knee with every step.
  • Sleep and mood. Poor sleep amplifies pain. The questionnaire asks about night pain for this reason.
  • Activity, not rest. For most knees, the right movement is medicine. Complete rest usually makes stiffness worse.

Your score

What the number means

Four bands, each with a clear next step. The bands are a guide, not a diagnosis. Sudden injury, locking, giving way or a hot swollen knee always deserve prompt medical review, whatever the score.

80–100
Healthy

Your knee is coping well. Keep active, keep strong, and retest if symptoms change.

60–79
Mild

Early symptoms. A structured six week strengthening programme resolves most knees in this band. Retest monthly.

40–59
Moderate

Symptoms are limiting daily life. See a physiotherapist or GP. Options include supervised rehabilitation, weight support and, for some, an injection.

0–39
Severe

Significant impact on daily life. Book a specialist review. Scores in this range are where joint replacement and other surgical options are discussed.

Track progress

One number, watched over time

A single score is a snapshot. A series of scores is a story. Retake the test every two to four weeks during treatment or recovery.

A rise of 10 points or more is a meaningful improvement. A steady fall is a signal to review your plan with a clinician.

Tip: write your score and date in your phone notes, or screenshot each result. Retesting takes five minutes.
